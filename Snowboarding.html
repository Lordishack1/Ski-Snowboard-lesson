Introduction to Snowboarding

A Basic Introduction to Snowboarding

Necessities

  • Snowboard
  • Snowboard Boots
  • Proper apparel (snow pants, coat, gloves, helment, lift ticket)

Parts of your Snowboard

  • Snowboard parts
    • Nose - This is the tip of the board that goes down the hill first
    • Tail - This is the back end of the board that is last down the hill
    • Toe side - This is the edge that your toes are closest too
    • Heel side - This is the edge that your heels are closest too
  • Binding parts
    • Highback - The back of your leg should line this
    • Angle strap - The top strap
    • Toe strap - The bottom strap
    • Footbed - Plant your foot here

Which foot first?

  • There is a little test that that you can do to easily see which foot your more comfortable with going first.
    • Stand on the side of the slope and face down hill.
    • Have someone push you slightly forward towards the bottom of the hill.
    • Which ever foot you step out with is the foot that you should have going down the hill first
  • Terminalogy
    • Goofy - Right foot first
    • Normal - Left foot first

Note: For this lesson you will be learning how to snowboard with one foot out.

Putting on your boots and Snowboard

  • Make sure your boots are tight against your feet
    • The inner linning or your boots should be snug before you tighen the out outside boot
  • Lay your snowboard out in front of you sideways accross the hill with the highback facing you

  • Line the back of your leg up with the high back in the binding
  • Strap up the top buckle across your ankle
  • Stap the bottom buckle arocss your toes
  • Now your all set!

Basic Controls

Beginning notes

  • Keep your weight on your front foot
    • It's a 90% front to 10% back weight distribution
  • Keep your shoulders parallel with your board
  • Always look at where your are going
    • Do not look at your board! Where you look is where you go
  • Stay confident that you can succeed, Snowboarding is more of a mind game so you have to have the confidence to continue with it
  • Most movements on a snowboard has to do with shifting your weight on to different parts of your front foot.

Movement across flat ground

  • In order to move across flat ground you have to have one foot out of the bindings planted in the snow behind your board.
  • With your front foot first, push forward from your front binding to your back binding with your free foot
  • At the same time slide your board with the push

Basic Stops

  1. Add weight to the back foot
  2. Kick out your back foot to the point where your board is sideways to the hill
  3. Pick up your down hill edge so you dont catch any snow
    • The more you dig in your up hill edge the more you will slow down
  4. Practicing stopping and going your desired speed until you are confident to slow yourself safely
    • If at any point you feel out of control fall to your side to stop yourself before any collisions occur

Basic Turns

Heelside turn

  1. Start sliding down the hill with your front foot first
  2. Act like you are going down to sit on a char with your hips
  3. Press on your front heel
    • Make sure your keep your legs nice and bent
  4. Hold the pressure in your heel to continue the turn

Toeside turn

  1. Start sliding down the hill with your front foot first
  2. Push our your front knee
  3. Apply weight to your front toes
    • Make sure your keep your legs nice and bent
  4. Hold the pressure in your toes to continue the turn

Next Steps, beginning advanced tips

Linking turns

  • Linking turns is another way of saying that you will be making turns across the hill with out stoping.
  1. Start with your most comfortable turn
  2. Instead of stopping let your board go flat when you are across the hill
  3. Engage your next turn
  4. Come to a complete stop
    • Once you are really comfortable with making one turn each way, see how many you can get!

FAQ/Troubleshooting

  • Q: Why am I not stopping?

  • A: You most likely don't have all your weight on your front foot. Try having some one take a video of you. If your hips are not over your front foot then all of your weight is still on your back foot

  • Q: Why am I not turning?

  • A: Try adding more weight to the front foot

  • Q: Why do I keep sitting down?

  • A: You still have all your weight on the back foot. All the control comes from your front foot.

  • Q: This guide is insufficient/inadequate, where can I learn more?

  • A: The internet has many resources, but the best practice is in person. Visit your local ski hill and ask about lessons, most places cater to most skill levels, but it varies.
